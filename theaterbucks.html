Bay Area TheaterBucks Proposal

I'm organizing a program I'll call "TheaterBucks" that would provide "eGift Cards" that could be redeemed at any participating East Bay 501(c)(3) community theater. The goal of this page is to provide enough information for you to decide if your theater might be interested in participating. If you're reading this, I assume you are a decision maker at a small-to-medium not-for-profit performing arts organization in the Bay Area.

The program wouldn't cost anything for theaters, nor require any changes to existing sales processes, as long as your point-of-sale system(s) allow "Card not present" (CNP) credit card transactions, for which the credit card number can be manually entered instead of requiring the card to be swiped.

How would patrons buy eGift cards?

They would buy online through a site run by a company called Yiftee (to which I have no connection but with whom I've discussed this program at length). The "home page" for the program would have the names and logos of theaters that accept the card, and a "TheaterBucks" branding (which I'm working on). The patron can use their credit card to buy an eGift card in any amount (up to a preset maximum we choose) for themselves, for a friend, etc. The recipient doesn't get a physical card; they get an email with (printable) redemption info as well as a link to check their balance anytime.

How would theaters redeem the cards?

The card is just a debit MasterCard that works only at designated vendors that can accept MasterCard "card not present" (CNP) purchases. they get "e-certificates" with the credit card info of this debit MasterCard (and some branding). Theaters would sign up to become a designated vendor. Then you would run it as a credit card (not debit or gift card) and key in the transaction like a phone order. Mastercard will validate. Do not go over the value of the card or the transaction will be declined.

What would be the cost to the theater?

Nothing. Yiftee makes money from the purchase fee and by getting a piece of the credit card fees you're already paying when the card is used. There's no separate cost or paperwork.

My theater already does gift cards--why would we do this?

Theater-specific cards are great gifts for your existing audience; this is a way to reach new audiences because they are not tied to one specific theater, but will still spend it on some participating theater(s). And more people going out to the theater is better for all theaters: a rising tide lifts all boats.

When are you planning to launch this?

Aiming for first quarter of 2023, or as soon as I get enough theaters on board to make the value proposition interesting to buyers!

What are the requirements for participating?

Other than being able to accept CNP transactions, we would ask your theater to "advertise" the card by placing a prominent link to it on your website (we can provide a clickable image and link), to have signage advertising the card at your box office (for walk-up sales, will-call, etc.), and ideally, plugging it during the curtain speech.

What range of value(s) would be available?

We can set whatever limit we want up to $250, though all participating theaters would be subject to the same limit. I was thinking $75-100, to cover the cost of two tickets to a performance and maybe a drink. The goal is to get new people in the door, then you can hook them on your theater by giving them collateral pieces when they check in.

Would the card work for online purchases and in-theater concessions?

It will work with anything your theater sells that handles CNP transactions, whether online or on-site. You have the option of only accepting it for ticket sales, but I don't recommend that restriction.

Can the theater sell or promote gift cards?

They can only be purchased online, but you can put a branded sign or poster with a QR code for customers who are interested, and you can "plug" them in your curtain speech. I would hope and expect that if theaters agree that selling more cards is great for getting more people into theaters, they would want to publicize/promote these in curtain speeches, program ads, email marketing, etc. I will provide sample text and collaterals that can be used as a starting point for this.

Can the theater check a customer's gift card balance?

The patron can check their own balance online, but there's no way for you to check it. If they try to pay for a transaction that exceeds the balance, it will be denied, but you won't have any way of knowing what the balance is.

What happens if a customer's card balance isn't enough to buy ticket(s)?

At your discretion, they can use the card for concessions or other items sold in-theater as long as those sales can be processed through a CNP transaction. Or, if your ticket system allows a single transaction to be split across multiple credit cards, they can use the gift card for the balance and pay the rest by some other means.

What analytics/reporting will my theater get that could be used for marketing purposes?

I'm working on the answer.

Could the "landing page" where people go to check balance, buy a card, etc. include content that changes periodically, like having links to the latest production's landing pages?

Yes, I'll work something out where we can roll in changes once a month or something.

Who makes money from this?

Yiftee, the company that administers the card, gets a fee when the card is purchased ($1 plus 5% of the card value), and gets a share of the credit card fees you're already paying. You make money when patrons use the card at your theater. Nobody else makes money, and certainly not me; I'm doing this because I am looking for more ways for theaters to bring in new audiences.

Can I apply a refund to the eGift Card?

Yes. Refunds can be applied to a valid (unexpired) card just as you would to a credit card.

Since the eGift Card is like a Mastercard, can it be redeemed anywhere?

No. They can only be redeemed at participating theaters.

Can the eGift Card be used more than once?

Yes. They are multi-use and the current balance and expiration date are always reflected on the digital voucher. Recipients receive monthly reminders to redeem.

For more details, read the complete merchant agreement, which will have to be signed by someone who can legally bind the theater in order to participate.
